# Optimization Model

This document describes the Mixed-Integer Linear Programming (MILP) model developed for the OptiSched project. The purpose of the model is to automatically generate feasible university course schedules by assigning subject offerings to qualified professors, classrooms, and time slots while satisfying all institutional constraints.

The optimization process is based on data managed by the OptiSched API, including professors, subject offerings, classrooms, time slots, qualifications, and availabilities. The resulting schedule must satisfy all hard constraints, such as professor qualifications, professor availability, classroom capacity, and resource exclusivity. Additional soft constraints may be incorporated into the objective function to improve the overall quality of the generated timetable.

The mathematical model is composed of the following elements:

- **Sets**, representing the entities involved in the scheduling process.
- **Parameters**, containing the input data extracted from the database.
- **Decision variables**, defining professor assignments and lecture allocations.
- **Constraints**, ensuring the feasibility of the generated schedule.
- **Objective function**, responsible for optimizing the quality of the timetable according to predefined criteria.

## Sets

The optimization model is defined over the following finite sets:

- \(P\): Set of professors.
- \(O\): Set of subject offerings to be scheduled.
- \(R\): Set of classrooms.
- \(T\): Set of available time slots.

---

## Parameters

The following parameters are extracted from the OptiSched database and define the scheduling instance.

### Subject Offering Parameters

- \(h_o\): Number of weekly time slots required by subject offering \(o \in O\).
- \(e_o\): Expected number of enrolled students.
- \(s_o\): Recommended semester.

### Classroom Parameters

- \(c_r\): Capacity of classroom \(r \in R\).

### Professor Parameters

- \(q_{po}\):

\[
q_{po}=
\begin{cases}
1,&\text{if professor }p\text{ is qualified to teach subject offering }o\\
0,&\text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\]

- \(a_{pt}\):

\[
a_{pt}=
\begin{cases}
1,&\text{if professor }p\text{ is available during time slot }t\\
0,&\text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\]

---

## Decision Variables

The optimization model uses two binary decision variables.

### Professor Assignment Variable

\[
y_{po}=
\begin{cases}
1,&\text{if professor }p\text{ is assigned to subject offering }o;\\
0,&\text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
\]

Each subject offering must be assigned to exactly one professor for the entire semester.

---

### Schedule Assignment Variable

\[
x_{port}=
\begin{cases}
1,&\text{if subject offering }o\text{ is scheduled}\\
&\text{with professor }p,\text{ in classroom }r,\\
&\text{during time slot }t;\\
0,&\text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
\]

Each variable with value 1 corresponds to a single `ScheduleEntry` generated by the optimization process.

The variables are linked through the model constraints, ensuring that every scheduled lecture is assigned to the unique professor selected for its corresponding subject offering.

## Constraints

The following constraints ensure the feasibility of the generated timetable.

---

### Subject Offering Constraints

#### (C1) Weekly Lecture Requirement

Each subject offering must be scheduled exactly the required number of weekly time slots.

\[
\sum_{p\in P}\sum_{r\in R}\sum_{t\in T}x_{port}=h_o,
\qquad
\forall o\in O
\]

---

#### (C2) Unique Professor Assignment

Each subject offering must be assigned to exactly one professor.

\[
\sum_{p\in P}y_{po}=1,
\qquad
\forall o\in O
\]

---

#### (C3) Professor Consistency

A lecture can only be assigned to the professor selected for its corresponding subject offering.

\[
x_{port}\le y_{po},
\qquad
\forall p\in P,\;
o\in O,\;
r\in R,\;
t\in T
\]

---

#### (C4) Course Conflict

Subject offerings belonging to the same course and recommended semester cannot be scheduled during the same time slot.

Define

\[
\delta_{oo'}=
\begin{cases}
1,&\text{if }o\text{ and }o'\text{ belong to the same course}\\
&\text{and recommended semester}\\
0,&\text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\]

Then,

\[
\sum_{p\in P}\sum_{r\in R}x_{port}
+
\sum_{p\in P}\sum_{r\in R}x_{po'rt}
\le1,
\]

\[
\forall t\in T,\;
\forall(o,o'):\delta_{oo'}=1
\]

Assumption: In the current MVP scope, every subject offering considered by the optimizer has a defined recommended_semester (mandatory/core courses only). Elective offerings without a recommended semester are out of scope and are not fed into the optimization engine at this stage. This assumption will be revisited when elective prioritization is introduced.

---

### Professor Constraints

#### (C5) Qualification

A professor can only be assigned to subject offerings for which they are qualified.

\[
y_{po}\le q_{po},
\qquad
\forall p\in P,\;
o\in O
\]

---

#### (C6) Availability

A lecture can only be scheduled during a professor's available time slots.

\[
x_{port}\le a_{pt},
\qquad
\forall p\in P,\;
o\in O,\;
r\in R,\;
t\in T
\]

---

#### (C7) Professor Exclusivity

A professor cannot teach more than one lecture during the same time slot.

\[
\sum_{o\in O}\sum_{r\in R}x_{port}
\le1,
\qquad
\forall p\in P,\;
t\in T
\]

---

### Classroom Constraints

#### (C8) Capacity

The assigned classroom must accommodate the expected number of students.

\[
e_o\,x_{port}\le c_r,
\qquad
\forall p\in P,\;
o\in O,\;
r\in R,\;
t\in T
\]

---

#### (C9) Classroom Exclusivity

A classroom cannot host more than one lecture during the same time slot.

\[
\sum_{p\in P}\sum_{o\in O}x_{port}
\le1,
\qquad
\forall r\in R,\;
t\in T
\]

---

#### (C10) Offering Time Slot Exclusivity

Each subject offering can occupy at most one classroom-professor combination per time slot.

\[
\sum_{p\in P}\sum_{r\in R}x_{port}\le1,
\qquad
\forall o\in O, t\in T
\]


---

### Time Slot Constraints

No additional hard constraints are required.

Each scheduled lecture occupies exactly one time slot by definition of the decision variable \(x_{port}\).

---

## Soft Constraints

The following requirements are modeled as optimization criteria and will be incorporated into the objective function.

### (S1) Minimize Timetable Holes

Minimize idle gaps between consecutive lectures assigned to the same professor.

---

### (S2) Distribute Lectures Along the Week

Prefer schedules in which the lectures of the same subject offering are distributed across different days whenever possible.

---

### (S3) Avoid Excessive Consecutive Lectures

Minimize long sequences of consecutive lectures assigned to the same professor.

---

### (S4) Classroom Stability

Prefer assigning all lectures of the same subject offering to the same classroom whenever possible.  

---

## Auxiliary Definitions for the Objective Function

Since the soft constraints depend on temporal structure (day grouping and slot ordering) that is not required by the hard constraints, the following auxiliary elements are introduced.

### Auxiliary Sets and Parameters

- \(D\): Set of days of the week spanned by \(T\).
- \(T_d \subseteq T\): Subset of time slots occurring on day \(d\in D\), ordered by start time.
- \(\text{pos}(t)\): Position of time slot \(t\) within its day (1-indexed), for \(t \in T_d\).
- \(\text{next}(t)\): The immediately following time slot on the same day as \(t\), if one exists.

---

### Auxiliary Variables

- \(z_{pt} = \sum_{o\in O}\sum_{r\in R}x_{port}\): Indicates whether professor (p) has a lecture during time slot (t). This variable is effectively binary as a consequence of (C7).
- \(f_{pd}, l_{pd} \ge 0\): First and last occupied slot position for professor \(p\) on day \(d\).
- \(w_{pd} \in {0,1}\): Indicates whether professor \(p\) has at least one lecture on day \(d\).
- \(g_{pt} \in {0,1}\), for \(t\) such that \(\text{next}(t)\) exists: Indicates a back-to-back lecture pair for professor \(p\) between \(t\) and \(\text{next}(t)\).
- \(v_{od} \in {0,1}\): Number of lectures of offering \(o\) scheduled on day \(d\), in excess of one.
- \(u_{or} \in {0,1}\): Indicates whether offering \(o\) uses classroom \(r\) at least once during the week.

---

### Auxiliary Constraints

#### (C11) Day Activity Linking
This constraint links the professor's hourly activity variable \(z_{pt}\) to the binary daily activity variable \(w_{pd}\). If the professor is scheduled to teach in any time slot $t$ belonging to day $d$, the model is forced to mark that the professor was active on that day by setting \(w_{pd} = 1\). 

\[
z_{pt} \le w_{pd}, \qquad \forall p\in P, d\in D, t\in T_d
\]

---

#### (C12) First/Last Slot Bounding
These constraints dynamically determine the position of the first $f_{pd}$ and last $l_{pd}$ active lecture slot for professor $p$ on day $d$. 

- The first inequality utilizes the Big-M technique (where $M$ is a sufficiently large constant, typically the maximum number of daily slots) to ensure that if $z_{pt} = 1$, the first active slot $f_{pd}$ is less than or equal to the current position $\text{pos}(t)$.
- The second inequality ensures that the last active slot $l_{pd}$ is greater than or equal to the position of any slot where the professor actually teaches that day.

\[
f_{pd} \le \text{pos}(t) + M(1-z_{pt}), \qquad
l_{pd} \ge \text{pos}(t)\cdot z_{pt},
\qquad \forall p\in P, d\in D, t\in T_d
\]

---

#### (C13) Consecutive Lecture Linking
This constraint tracks back-to-back lecture blocks for a professor on the same day. If professor $p$ teaches in slot $t$ ($z_{pt} = 1$) and also in the immediately following slot $\text{next}(t)$ ($z_{p,\text{next}(t)} = 1$), the sum on the right-hand side forces the consecutive indicator variable $g_{pt}$ to assume the value 1. 

\[
g_{pt} \ge z_{pt} + z_{p,\text{next}(t)} - 1,
\qquad \forall p\in P, t\in T:\text{next}(t)\text{ exists}
\]

--- 

#### (C14) Same-Day Concentration
This inequality calculates the excess lectures of a single subject offering ($o$) scheduled on the same day $d$. The variable $v_{od}$ represents the number of lectures the offering has on that day beyond the first one (e.g., if there are 2 lectures on the same day, $v_{od} \ge 2 - 1 = 1$; if there is only 1 lecture, $v_{od} \ge 1 - 1 = 0$).

\[
v_{od} \ge \sum_{t\in T_d}\sum_{p\in P}\sum_{r\in R}x_{port} - 1,
\qquad \forall o\in O, d\in D
\]

---

#### (C15) Classroom Usage Linking
This constraint binds the schedule assignment ($x_{port}$) to the classroom usage indicator variable ($u_{or}$). If subject offering $o$ is scheduled in classroom $r$ during any time slot of the semester, the model is forced to record that classroom $r$ was used by that offering ($u_{or} = 1$).

\[
x_{port} \le u_{or}, \qquad \forall p\in P, o\in O, r\in R, t\in T
\]

---

## Objective Function

The objective function combines the four soft constraints into a single weighted minimization term. Each component penalizes an undesirable scheduling pattern; weights \(\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta \ge 0\) control the relative importance of each criterion and are calibrated experimentally.

### (S1) Timetable Holes
Calculates the total idle time (empty windows) that professors spend at the university. For each active day and professor, the actual number of lectures taught ($\sum z_{pt}$) is subtracted from the total span between their first and last lectures of the day ($l_{pd} - f_{pd} + 1$). The resulting value represents the number of empty slots ("holes") between their scheduled classes. 

\[
\text{Holes} = \sum_{p\in P}\sum_{d\in D}\Big[(l_{pd}-f_{pd}+1) - \sum_{t\in T_d}z_{pt}\Big]
\]

---

### (S2) Distribution Along the Week
Penalizes scheduling multiple lectures of the same offering on a single day. Minimizing this term distributes the lectures of a subject across different days of the week (e.g., splitting a 4-credit course into two 2-credit classes on different days), which is pedagogically preferred.

\[
\text{Concentration} = \sum_{o\in O}\sum_{d\in D} v_{od}
\]

---

### (S3) Excessive Consecutive Lectures
Penalizes long sequences of consecutive lectures assigned to the same professor. Minimizing this term prevents teacher fatigue by discouraging the scheduling of long, uninterrupted teaching blocks.

\[
\text{Consecutiveness} = \sum_{p\in P}\sum_{t\in T:,\text{next}(t)\text{ exists}} g_{pt}
\]

---

### (S4) Classroom Stability
Measures the number of additional classrooms a subject offering uses throughout the week beyond the first one. If the offering uses only one classroom ($r$), then $\sum_{r} u_{or} = 1$, which zeroes out the penalty. If it uses multiple rooms, the term increases linearly, penalizing the lack of physical stability for the students. 

\[
\text{ClassroomChanges} = \sum_{o\in O}\Big(\sum_{r\in R}u_{or} - 1\Big)
\]

---

### Combined Objective
The final multi-objective model is formulated as a single weighted sum minimization function. The solver seeks to find a feasible solution that minimizes the cumulative cost of all soft constraints:

$$\begin{aligned}
\min \quad & \alpha \cdot \text{Holes} \ + \\
           & \beta \cdot \text{Concentration} \ + \\
           & \gamma \cdot \text{Consecutiveness} \ + \\
           & \delta \cdot \text{ClassroomChanges}
\end{aligned}$$

$$\text{subject to constraints } (C1) \text{ to } (C15).$$
